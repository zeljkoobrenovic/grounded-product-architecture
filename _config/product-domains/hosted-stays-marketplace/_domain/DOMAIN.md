# Hosted Stays Marketplace

This domain models Airbnb's clearest core business: a hosted-stays marketplace that matches guests with homes, rooms, and professionally managed short-term rentals through search, listing trust, booking, messaging, payouts, and risk controls.

Why this domain:

- `airbnb.com` is still most strongly identified with hosted accommodations, even as Airbnb expands adjacent services and experiences.
- Airbnb's operating model is differentiated by host-led supply, listing quality, pricing/availability control, guest-host trust, and issue recovery rather than hotel-style distribution alone.
- Public Airbnb materials continue to emphasize hosts, guest arrivals, AirCover, pricing tools, co-hosting, and listing performance as core marketplace loops.

Research summary used on 2026-03-31:

- Airbnb Investor Relations, February 13, 2025:
  - Airbnb reported it had grown to over 5 million hosts and over 2 billion guest arrivals.
- Airbnb 2025 SEC reporting and shareholder materials:
  - 2025 growth remained driven by Nights and Experiences Booked and Gross Booking Value.
  - Q4 2025 shareholder materials highlighted app share at 64% of nights booked and pay-later adoption above 70% of eligible bookings.
- Airbnb hosting resource center, updated in 2025:
  - Airbnb publicly emphasizes pricing tools, calendar control, discounts, Superhost status, and faster response behavior as drivers of host performance.
  - Airbnb states Superhost status improves guest confidence and that over 1.3 million hosts had become Superhosts.
- Airbnb help and host-protection materials, updated in 2025:
  - AirCover for Hosts includes $1 million host liability insurance and up to $3 million host damage protection.
  - Airbnb's reservation screening, identity verification, and support protections are positioned as first-class marketplace trust features.
- Airbnb fee guidance, updated August 25, 2025:
  - Stay monetization is still anchored in service fees, with common split-fee patterns of about 3% host fee and roughly 14.1% to 16.5% guest fee in eligible setups.

Domain hypothesis:

1. Guest conversion depends on strong search relevance, distinctive listing quality, and trust signals that reduce booking regret.
2. Host supply quality depends on fast activation, simple calendar/pricing control, and confidence in payout, protection, and support policies.
3. Professional host growth depends on operational tooling, co-host permissions, and multi-listing performance management.
4. Marketplace defensibility depends on identity, risk screening, AirCover, payments, policy enforcement, and data-driven ranking.
5. Airbnb's next layer of growth can expand from the hosted-stays core, but the core still starts with bookable nights, high-quality listings, and reliable host economics.
