# Freight Logistics Orchestration

This product domain models the business operated by Uber Freight, C.H. Robinson, Ryder Logistics, and adjacent freight technology and third-party logistics providers.

The selected domain is freight logistics orchestration because the referenced companies all help shippers move goods through a mix of managed transportation, freight brokerage, carrier networks, transportation management systems, contract logistics, dedicated transportation, and operational control towers. The domain is broader than pure digital brokerage: shippers need planning, procurement, tendering, execution, visibility, exception management, freight audit, settlement, and performance optimization across many modes and partners.

Structural references used while creating this domain:

- `local-on-demand-delivery-marketplace` for multi-sided marketplace/customer, product brick, product capability, delivery, and objective structures.
- `online-retail-marketplace` for marketplace trust, catalog, payment, operations, and portfolio conventions.
- `enterprise-crm-and-revenue-operations` for B2B operational personas, team ownership, and platform foundation patterns.

Research assumptions:

- Public companies report freight revenue and operating metrics at different levels of granularity. Competition statistics preserve the reported scope instead of normalizing them into a narrower domain claim.
- Private or subsidiary businesses such as Uber Freight and Flexport disclose fewer domain-pure metrics. Where official operating metrics are available, they are included; otherwise competition entries use official links and omit unsupported scale claims.
- KPI target values are realistic seed targets for a modeled product domain, informed by public freight brokerage, logistics, and marketplace reporting, not official targets from the named companies.
