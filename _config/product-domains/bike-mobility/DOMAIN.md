Bike Mobility Services (BMS) aims to develop sustainable and affordable bike mobility services for companies and their employees. Brands like Lease a Bike, B2Bike, BusinessBike, Movelo and FietsNED make mobility fun and easy by offering fully digitized services and collaborating with bike dealers and other service partners. Together, our BMS brands serve more than 20,000 companies and 200,000 people worldwide.

The Mission

Sustainability, urbanization, and electrification are reshaping the world. Bike Mobility Services (BMS) is at the forefront of this transformation. The company’s ambition is bold and clear: to get every employee in Europe on a bike.

As a unique joint venture between Pon.Bike, the world’s largest bicycle manufacturer, and Volkswagen Financial Services, BMS operates as the B2B powerhouse behind leading mobility labels such as Lease a Bike, BusinessBike, and B2Bike.

Through a sophisticated digital platform, the organization connects more than 100,000 companies, 15,000+ dealers, and hundreds of employees across Europe.



A Decade of Growth

In under a decade, BMS has grown from a German start‑up into a highly profitable, multi‑country mobility and leasing platform, active across Europe. With strong financial health, a powerful shareholder structure, and a technology-enabled business model, the company is now entering its next chapter of international expansion.



From Scale-up to Global Platform

BMS is embarking on a transformative growth phase that will reshape its operating model, technology landscape, and international footprint:

• From scale-up → to an integrated international platform group

• From entrepreneurial build-up → to a professionalised, repeatable operating model

• From organic growth → to systematic M&A and ecosystem expansion

• From fragmented systems → to a coherent, scalable platform architecture.

Technology, platforms, and data are becoming core value drivers—enabling international scale, M&A velocity, ecosystem control, remarketing, insurance, and captive leasing. What once served as support functions are now central to the company’s competitive advantage and future growth.

This next phase requires strong technical leadership to provide clear direction, near‑term stabilisation, and the ability to orchestrate ecosystem‑wide complexity.
