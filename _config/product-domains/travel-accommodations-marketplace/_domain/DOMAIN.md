# Travel Accommodations Marketplace

## Why this domain exists

This domain models the core accommodations marketplace in which Booking.com most clearly operates today: matching global travel demand with hotel, apartment, vacation-rental, hostel, and other stay supply through a two-sided platform with search, ranking, checkout, reviews, payments, servicing, and partner operations.

Accommodation is the most suitable Booking.com product domain to model because it remains the company's clearest marketplace core, the largest supply surface, and the anchor for many connected-trip motions.

## Research summary

The model was informed by current public sources reviewed on 2026-03-31:

- Booking.com "How we work" page, updated May 31, 2025:
  - Booking.com states it provides the platform, while the accommodation provider delivers the travel experience.
  - It explains that accommodation payments may happen at the property, be charged in advance by the property, or be organized by Booking.com or an affiliate.
  - It describes that accommodation recommendations are influenced by factors such as popularity, conversion, quality, commission, payouts, availability, and participation in programs such as Genius or Preferred.
  - It states that reviews come from customers who booked through the platform and are generally shown for 36 months.
- Booking.com partner onboarding pages:
  - Supply spans hotels, apartments, holiday homes, guest houses, and B&Bs.
  - Hosts can choose instant booking or Request to Book in some flows.
  - Booking.com facilitates partner payments and promotes daily payouts.
  - Public partner messaging emphasizes first-booking speed, property protection, and global demand access.
- Booking.com developer documentation:
  - Connectivity and demand APIs expose accommodation search, availability, reviews, reservation, and inventory workflows for ecosystem partners.
- Booking Holdings 2024 results and corporate materials:
  - Booking Holdings continues to describe accommodations as the largest part of the business, with Booking.com as the primary global accommodation marketplace brand.
  - Connected-trip expansion exists, but accommodation supply, room nights, conversion, payments, and partner monetization remain core economics.

## Domain hypothesis

A Booking.com-style accommodations marketplace competes on five connected value pools:

1. Traveler demand capture through destination discovery, trust, and high-conversion search.
2. Booking conversion through transparent policies, pricing, payments, and low-friction checkout.
3. Stay confidence through reviews, support, self-service changes, and disruption recovery.
4. Partner yield and supply quality through pricing, availability, merchandising, and payout operations.
5. Platform defensibility through trust, payments, connectivity, and marketplace intelligence.

## Modeling choices

- The domain is intentionally generic rather than Booking.com-branded, but it is grounded in Booking.com operating reality.
- It models both traditional accommodation partners and alternative-accommodation hosts because Booking.com publicly serves both at scale.
- Managed travel is included as a demand-side persona because Booking.com supports business travel and policy-constrained booking flows, but it is not treated as the primary commercial center of gravity.
- Connectivity partners are modeled through products, teams, and capabilities rather than as the main customer narrative because they are critical enablers of supply scale, not the dominant value narrative.
