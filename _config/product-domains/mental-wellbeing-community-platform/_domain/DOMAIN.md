# Mental Wellbeing Community Platform

This domain models `cadre.io` as a multi-sided mental wellbeing community platform rather than a teletherapy marketplace or a generic HR benefits portal.

Why this domain:

- `cadre.io` positions itself as "the social platform for better mental health" with individual, employer, and contributor paths.
- The public product combines always-on community access, live sessions, on-demand content, privacy-friendly participation, contributor supply, and employer-sponsored team access.
- The employer motion is important, but the product system still centers on member engagement and contributor-led support rather than only on benefits administration.

Research summary used on 2026-04-16:

- Cadre homepage, accessed April 16, 2026:
  - Cadre describes itself as a social platform for better mental health with paths for individuals, employers, and contributors.
- Cadre features page, accessed April 16, 2026:
  - Public feature claims emphasize daily livestreams, on-demand content, community, AI-powered recommendations, reminders, and privacy-friendly handling.
- Cadre employers page, accessed April 16, 2026:
  - Cadre is positioned as an employer mental health benefit and alternative or complement to traditional EAP usage, with a published testimonial that 98% of employees found the benefit useful at TPI Hospitality.
- Cadre plans page, accessed April 16, 2026:
  - Public pricing shows free starter access and paid individual and team plans at $4 per month on annual billing.
- Cadre live events and onboarding pages, accessed April 16, 2026:
  - Public materials show custom employer events, access codes, community/topic access, anonymity guidance, and references to vetted peers, professionals, and one-to-one care booking in some onboarding flows.

Domain hypothesis:

1. Cadre wins when it reduces time-to-support and makes mental wellbeing engagement feel socially safe, low-friction, and repeatable.
2. Employer retention depends less on procurement polish alone and more on activation, usefulness, and visible employee engagement after launch.
3. Contributor quality and consistency are strategic because daily live sessions, trusted content, and community safety rely on durable supply.
4. Privacy, moderation, and escalation are foundational because the product works in a sensitive mental health context with pseudonymous participation and employer-sponsored access.
5. Cadre's differentiation is the combined system of community, content, live support, contributor trust, and employer deployment rather than a single clinical workflow.
